# LifeOS Passkeys (FIDO2/WebAuthn)

LifeOS leverages **Passkeys**, built on the FIDO2 and WebAuthn standards, to provide a highly secure, phishing-resistant, and user-friendly authentication experience. Passkeys replace traditional passwords with cryptographic key pairs, eliminating common vulnerabilities associated with passwords.

## What are Passkeys?

Passkeys are a new standard for passwordless authentication. Instead of a password, a passkey is a cryptographic key pair generated by your device. The public key is stored with LifeOS, and the private key remains securely on your device (e.g., smartphone, hardware security key, computer's secure enclave).

When you log in, your device uses the private key to prove your identity to LifeOS without ever sending the private key itself. This process is:

-   **Phishing-Resistant**: Because there's no password to type, there's nothing for a phishing site to steal.
-   **Stronger Security**: Passkeys use strong cryptography, making them virtually impossible to guess or crack.
-   **User-Friendly**: Authentication often involves a simple biometric scan (fingerprint, face ID) or a PIN, making login faster and more convenient.
-   **Cross-Device Sync**: Passkeys can be synchronized across your devices (e.g., via iCloud Keychain, Google Password Manager), making them accessible wherever you need them.

## How LifeOS Uses Passkeys

LifeOS integrates Passkeys as the primary method for **Identity Trust** within its Zero Trust Architecture. This means:

1.  **Registration**: When you register a Passkey with LifeOS, your device generates a unique cryptographic key pair. The public key is securely stored by LifeOS, linked to your user account.
2.  **Authentication**: To log in, you simply confirm your identity on your device (e.g., with a fingerprint). Your device then uses the private key to sign a challenge from LifeOS, proving your identity without ever exposing your private key.
3.  **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Built-in**: Passkeys inherently provide two factors of authentication: something you have (your device with the private key) and something you are (your biometric) or something you know (your device PIN).
4.  **Recovery Keys**: In case you lose all your devices with Passkeys, LifeOS provides a secure mechanism for recovery using pre-generated recovery codes, ensuring you never lose access to your LifeOS.

## Passkey Manager (`identity/passkey_manager.py`)

The `PasskeyManager` module handles the lifecycle of Passkeys within LifeOS:

-   **`register_passkey(user_id: str, device_name: str) -> Dict[str, Any]`**: Simulates the registration of a new Passkey, generating a unique credential ID and public key for the user.
-   **`authenticate(user_id: str, credential_id: str) -> bool`**: Simulates the authentication process, verifying the provided credential ID against registered Passkeys and updating usage statistics.
-   **`get_user_passkeys(user_id: str) -> List[Dict[str, Any]]`**: Retrieves a list of all Passkeys registered for a specific user, including metadata like device name, creation date, and last usage.

## WebAuthn Standard

LifeOS's Passkey implementation adheres to the **WebAuthn (Web Authentication)** standard, which is a core component of FIDO2. WebAuthn is a web API that allows web applications to integrate strong, passwordless, and phishing-resistant authentication directly into browsers and operating systems. This ensures broad compatibility and a standardized approach to secure authentication.

By adopting Passkeys and WebAuthn, LifeOS provides a state-of-the-art authentication experience that is both highly secure and incredibly convenient for users.
